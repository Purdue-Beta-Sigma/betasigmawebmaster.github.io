History

National History

Tau Beta Sigma traces its origins to Texas Technological College (now Texas Tech University) in 1939, when bandswomen Wava Banes, Emily SoRelle, and Ruth La Nell Williams envisioned an organization dedicated to supporting women in collegiate bands. Inspired by the mission of Kappa Kappa Psi, they sought to create a sorority that would recognize leadership and service while also providing educational, social, and personal development opportunities for women musicians.

Over the next several years, the women at Texas Tech developed the organization's symbols, traditions, ritual, and governing documents. As plans for a national sorority took shape, the Texas Tech members worked closely with leaders of Kappa Kappa Psi and collaborated with a similar women's band organization at Oklahoma A&M College (now Oklahoma State University). In an effort to establish the strongest possible national foundation, the Texas Tech chapter generously transferred the name Tau Beta Sigma and much of its organizational work to the Oklahoma A&M group so that the national charter could be filed in Oklahoma. As a result, the Oklahoma A&M chapter became the Alpha Chapter, while Texas Tech—recognized as the birthplace of the Sorority and home of its earliest development—became the Beta Chapter. The agreement also designated Wava Banes as the Founder of Tau Beta Sigma and ensured that the first National President would come from the Texas Tech chapter.

On March 26, 1946, Tau Beta Sigma was officially chartered as a national organization. Since that time, the Sorority has grown to serve college and university bands across the United States. Guided by its enduring principles of leadership, service, and sisterhood, Tau Beta Sigma continues to support and strengthen the collegiate band experience for generations of musicians.

If you are interested in learning more, you can visit the TBS National Website!

Beta Sigma

The Beta Sigma chapter at Purdue University was founded on April 12, 1959 where it became the 66th chapter of Tau Beta Sigma. Founding chapter members included Joyce Wimmer, Eleanor Rotz, Sandra Hutchinson, Phillis Baker, Connie Crook, Frances Eisenberg, Eleanor Glanville, and Diana Rhinebeck. Our big sister chapter, Rho, is close by at Indiana University. They are also our little sister chapter as Beta Sigma helped with Rho's re-installation in 1974. Our current chapter sponsor is Dr. Lucas Petersen, Clinical Assistant Professor with Purdue Bands & Orchestras and we are very greatful to have his support. Since our founding, the chapter has grown from its original 8 to a wonderful 45 active members and continues to expand each year.

Joint Relations

Beta Sigma is proud to have a very close relationship with the other service organizations at Purdue, including Kappa Kappa Psi and the Volunteer Management Teams. Purdue's Gamma Pi became the 88th chapter of Kappa Kappa Psi on March 18, 1956.
